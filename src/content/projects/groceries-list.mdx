## Overview

Most grocery list apps want your attention. Groceries List just wants to help you get to the store.

Built by developer Nick Frost with a straightforward philosophy — no ads, no data harvesting, no unnecessary features — Groceries List needed a brand identity that reflected that same clarity. Simple, clean, and just enough personality to feel human.

---

## The Brief

Nick came to me with an informal brief rooted in a specific frustration: most utility apps are cluttered with advertising and quietly collect more user data than they need. Groceries List was his answer to that — a focused, privacy-conscious alternative that did one thing well.

The brand needed to match that philosophy. It couldn't feel corporate or generic, but it also couldn't be so playful that it undermined the simplicity at the core of the product.

---

## The Design Process

My initial concepts leaned more iconographic and expressive — a range of playful directions that explored the personality end of the spectrum. Nick's feedback was clear: pull it back. The product's strength was its restraint, and the brand needed to reflect that.

That redirection was the right call. It pushed me toward a solution that was harder to achieve — something that felt warm and approachable without relying on visual complexity to get there.

The final mark paired a vector blueberry with a thick, clean sans-serif logotype. The blueberry is small and friendly — just enough personality to signal that this isn't a corporate product — while the typography carries the clarity and weight the brand needed. Together they sit at exactly the balance Nick was after: simple, clean, and quietly human.

---

## Light & Dark

One of the more interesting constraints was designing for both light and dark themes simultaneously. Rather than designing one and adapting the other, I built both in parallel — ensuring the brand felt intentional and cohesive in either context, not like one was an afterthought.

This approach kept the color palette honest. Every choice had to work in both environments, which reinforced the simplicity of the system and prevented decisions that would only hold up in one mode.

---

## The Result

Groceries List has been live since 2021 with a brand that hasn't needed to change. The identity has held up across years of active use — a quiet sign that the foundational decisions were sound.

The app remains what it set out to be: a clean, ad-free, privacy-respecting tool that just helps you make a list. The brand says exactly that.

---

## Reflection

This project was a good reminder that restraint is its own creative discipline. My instinct was to bring more — more personality, more visual interest, more distinctiveness. The right answer was less, and getting there required letting go of ideas I genuinely liked in favor of what the product actually needed.

Designing for someone else's vision, especially when it differs from your own initial direction, is a skill. This project sharpened it.
