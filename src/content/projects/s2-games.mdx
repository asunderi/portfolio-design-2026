## Overview

S2 Games was my introduction to the games industry — and to what it means to work as a creative on a live, ambitious product with a passionate team behind it. In eighteen months I moved across two roles, two departments, and two entirely different creative disciplines, leaving with a foundation I still draw from today.

---

## Chapter One — World Building & Content (2013)

I joined S2 Games in early 2013 as part of the Mobile Department, working on an untitled mobile game in early development. Alongside one other team member, I was responsible for the creative foundation of the game itself — story, character design, and world building.

### What the Role Involved

- Developing narrative, lore, and the fictional world the game inhabited
- Designing characters and establishing their visual and story identities
- Managing external contractors producing 2D character designs and 3D models
- Coordinating asset delivery and maintaining creative consistency across contributors

Being one of two people responsible for an entire game world is a significant creative responsibility. It required thinking at two levels simultaneously — the big picture of a cohesive world, and the granular detail of individual characters and their stories.

This chapter taught me something I've carried into every design role since: **good design starts with understanding the story being told.**

---

## Chapter Two — Graphic Designer, Strife (2014)

In early 2014 I transitioned into a Graphic Designer role, joining a small web team of three working on Strife — S2 Games' flagship MOBA. The team was tight, talented, and generous with their knowledge. I absorbed as much as I could.

The work spanned web, print, email, and brand — often simultaneously.

### Web Design

Working across the Strife website I designed hero pages, landing pages, and player-facing interfaces. I used APIs to pull live hero data and statistics into informational web interfaces, creating pages that felt connected to the game rather than just describing it.

The standard was high — pixel-perfect mockups, strict feedback, and a team that cared deeply about craft. It raised my bar considerably.

### Email Marketing

In collaboration with the Marketing department I designed and developed a multi-campaign email series tied to Strife's launch — beta invites, feedback requests, welcome emails, and player milestone communications.

Each email was designed to feel like an extension of the game itself, using in-game assets and concept art to create immersive scenes rather than standard marketing layouts. The campaigns ran in two distinct styles: one clean and accessible, one richly game-like — tailored to different audience moments in the player journey.

This project was my first experience working with dynamic variables in email development. The Marketing team brought me into a technical training session for their platform — one of the more memorable cross-department collaborations of my time there.

### PAX Prime 2014 — Large Format & Event Materials

For S2 Games' presence at PAX Prime 2014, I designed the full suite of event and large-format materials representing Strife at one of North America's largest gaming conventions:

- Building awning banners and billboard graphics
- Lightning banners and kiosk displays
- Convention booth graphics and display materials
- Flyers, table tents, and event handouts

PAX Prime was my first large-format print experience. Designing for a building-scale banner is a different discipline entirely — viewing distance, color profiles, production specs, and the single job of stopping someone mid-crowd all have to be held in mind at once. Working under a real deadline for a real event accelerated that learning quickly.

### Logo Design — Prophecy of Darkstone

I designed the logo for *Child of the Dawn* — the first episode of Strife's single-player mini-adventure series, Prophecy of Darkstone. The process involved multiple iterations with careful attention to maintaining alignment with Strife's existing brand identity, which was notable in the gaming space for its clean, minimal aesthetic.

Navigating the feedback process on this project was its own education. The direction could be direct and difficult to parse — but it taught me to separate tone from substance, extract the actionable insight, and keep refining. That skill has served me in every client relationship since.

## Reflection

S2 Games was formative in ways that are hard to fully articulate. I joined as someone learning what design could be, and left with a genuine understanding of craft, collaboration, and creative accountability.

The web team that welcomed me — small, skilled, and generous — shaped how I think about working with others. The world building chapter shaped how I think about narrative and meaning in design. Together, they set a standard I've been building toward ever since.
